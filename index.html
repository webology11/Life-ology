Bacteria are tiny living micro-organisms that can be found everywhere; most are harmless or helpful (example: lactobacillus), but some can cause diseases.

What Are Bacterial Diseases?

Bacterial diseases occur when harmful bacteria enter the body, multiply, release toxins and hence disrupt the normal body functions. They spread through water, air, food, surfaces, and physical contact.

üíßü§¢ 1. Cholera

The Disease : Cholera is a bacterial disease that causes severe watery diarrhoea.

Cause : Caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. Spreads through unhealthy street food, contaminated water and food, and flies.

Incubation Period : A few hours to 6 days.

Symptoms : Vomiting, Loose motion, Dehydration, Abdominal pain, Very little or no urination because of shortage of water in the body Urea accumulates in the body which is poisonous Can be fatal if not treated.

Cure : Medicinal injection to supply water to the blood. Doctors give saline water injection.

Prevention :Good sanitation. Keeping food covered to prevent flies. Boiling water for drinking. Cholera can be treated by an anti-cholera injection.

ü§íüç≤ 2. Typhoid Fever

The Disease : Typoid fever is a bacterial infection caused by Salmonella typhi. It mainly affects the intestines and bloodsteams, leading to high fever and weakness.

Cause : Caused by the flagellated bacterium Salmonella typhi. It is spread by contaminated water and food, and poor hygiene.

Incubation Period : Usually 7-14 days after infection. Can extend up to 30 days in some cases. During this time, bacteria multiply silently in the intestines and bloodstream.

Symptoms : Continuous high fever, rising in afternoon/evening. Loss of appetite. Reddish erruptios or rashes on chest abdomen. Relapse may occur after recovery.

Cure : Treated with antibiotics such as Chloramphenicol (older treatment), Ceftriaxone, Azithromycin, etc. Plenty of fluids and rest.

Prevention : Typhoid vaccination, Clean drinking water, Good hygiene, avoiding raw/unclean street food, Control of flies to avoid contamination.

üò∑ü§ß 3. Tuberculosis (TB)

The Disease : Tuberculosis is a disease of lungs. In severe cases other parts may also be affected.

Cause : Caused by mycobacterium Tuberculosis - small rod shaped bacillus bacterium. Spread through the sputum , germs present in air.

Incubation Period : 2-10 weeks.

Symptoms : cough, afternoon fever, bloody mucus, loss of weight, fatigue, chest pain, and breathlessness.

Prevention : streptomycin antibiotic, BCG (bacillus calmette guerin) vaccine, and isolation of patient.
